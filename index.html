EEG

(Electroencephalography)

EEG analysis is a fascinating field that delves into the electrical activity of the brain. It uses a non-invasive technique to record electrical signals generated by the synchronized firing of neurons in the brain's cortex. By analyzing these recordings, we can gain valuable insights into various aspects of brain function.

ECG

(Electrocardiogram)

ECG is a fundamental tool used to assess the electrical activity of the heart. It's a non-invasive, painless procedure that provides valuable insights into heart function, rhythm, and potential abnormalities.

ANC

(Active Noise Control)

Active noise control, also sometimes called noise cancellation or active noise reduction (ANC), is a technology used to reduce unwanted background noise. It works by using microphones to pick up external sounds, then generating a sound wave that specifically cancels out those unwanted noises.
